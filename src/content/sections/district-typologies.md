The 2,200-unit redensification zone is not uniform. Three district typologies create variety and respond to their specific edge conditions.

## Boulevard District

The densest zone, lining both sides of the new boulevard:

- 4-story mixed-use: retail and services at ground floor, apartments above
- Building facades at the property line create a continuous street wall with pedestrian shade
- Upper floors step back to reduce visual mass and allow daylight
- BR (Business Residential) zoning permits this mixed-use flexibility
- Unit mix: primarily stacked apartments (35%) and mixed-use (10%)
- Rear parking accessed from side streets, not the boulevard face

## Riparian Edge District

The transition zone between boulevard intensity and stream corridor ecology:

- Buildings step down from 4 stories (at boulevard interface) to 2-3 stories at the stream edge
- Predominantly residential with individual ground-floor entries
- Larger setbacks toward the stream allow vegetated stormwater features — the riparian buffer's ecological function extends into the built district
- Daylight and ventilation studies ensure the stream corridor receives adequate sunlight for ecosystem health
- Unit mix: townhomes (25%) with some stacked apartments
- Quiet, green character — the neighborhood counterpoint to the boulevard's urban energy

## Coastal Edge District

The makai (oceanward) edge where the town meets the coastal buffer:

- 3-story maximum — preserving ocean view corridors and limiting shadow on the coastal park
- Community-oriented ground floors: gathering spaces, cultural facilities, small-scale retail
- Residential units on upper floors positioned outside the SLR-XA flood exposure zone
- Walking distance to heritage sites: Banyan Tree, Moku'ula, historic churches
- Cultural continuity is the design principle — new buildings reference Lahaina's architectural character (single-wall wood-frame, corrugated metal roofing, covered lanai at minimum 6-foot depth, 35-foot height limit per the Architectural Style Book)
- View corridors maintained to ocean and to West Maui Mountains

## Why Three Types Matter

A single housing typology — say, all 4-story apartments — would efficiently house everyone but would create a monotonous, placeless district. The three typologies produce a neighborhood with distinct characters: the active boulevard, the quiet riparian edge, and the culturally rich coastal edge. Each responds to its specific environmental and social conditions rather than imposing a uniform solution.

The height caps (4/3/2-3 stories) are not arbitrary. They follow the West Maui Community Plan guidelines and are constrained by Type V wood-frame construction limits — avoiding the cost premium of steel or concrete structural systems that would make affordable housing impossible.
