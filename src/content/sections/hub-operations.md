## Hub 1: Neighborhood Center with Primary School

There's a neighborhood center that mixes with a primary school. It's right on the spine where Lahaina Luna Avenue crosses the New Blvd. This places the hub right in the middle of a redensification zone with people. It is perfect for its aggregate and its facilities to surrounding residential districts and promoting development, too.

The primary school acts as the first hub and anchors how the neighborhood works every day. Families with kids need to live close to educational facilities. The neighborhood center is there for flexible community space like meeting rooms, cultural programming, and activities after primary school. This extends the building usefulness even when primary school is out. In emergency conditions, the hub converts a shelter and distribution point. Its gymnasium, cafeteria, and open circulation spaces accommodating displaced residents and relief operations. The neighborhood center also has roles like first response and can even serve as a hospital if needed. So, it is a hub for both daily life and emergency situations in the community.

## Hub 2: Health Services and First Response

The second hub integrates a neighborhood center with first response facilities and health services. It's located in the northern part of the redensification zone. This spot fills a critical gap that showed up during the 2023 fire. The problem was Lahaina didn't have nearby emergency medical capacity, so injured people transport delays, which took too long. Now, this health services offers urgent care, primary care clinics, and pharmacy access for daily needs. This means reducing vehicle trips to distant medical facilities. Plus, having fire and emergency medical services together right here shortening response times in the re-densified core. The neighborhood center is a community gathering space. When things are normal, it offers stuff like health education programming, senior services, and social support functions. But emergencies, it turns into a medical triage and coordination point. It's direct access the boulevard corridor so ambulance staging and evacuation.

## Hub 3: Community Center with First Response

There's also a third hub that connects this community center with first response facilities. It's located at the Eastern extent of the new Boulevard. This setup makes sure that emergency response can happen all along the boulevard corridor.

This hub a backup for emergencies. The neighborhood center helps the Coastal Edge District and the heritage zone. It's a neighborhood center where people gather for things like cultural programming and visitor services because its proximity to the coastal promenade and old sites. The first response component makes sure they capacity fire suppression and medical emergencies fast. They have easy access to both the main road and Wainee Street, which means they capacity get to the waterfront area quickly when needed.

## Distribution Logic

The three hubs are set up so everyone in the town is just a five-minute walk from one of them.

These nodes also catalysts adjacent development. When public investment is spent in certain places along the boulevard, it gives private development more confidence. They orient where services will be available, so they reducing risk starting residential and commercial projects there. This accelerating to re-densification timeline. The primary school hub shows a strong hub for sticking with family-oriented housing. It counters that recovery development doesn't just cater to tourists or short-term uses. Instead, it focuses on permanent resident needs.
