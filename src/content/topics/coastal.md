---
title: "Coastal"
summary: "Sea level rise, managed retreat, and the transformation of Front Street into a public promenade."
---

Lahaina's coastline faces accelerating change. At 3.2 feet of sea level rise, approximately 3,130 acres of West Maui coastal land face chronic flooding, with 780 structures and an estimated 1,600 residents at risk of displacement independent of fire damage. The recovery framework proposes a coastal buffer based on partial retreat — new permanent residential construction is prohibited within the SLR-XA exposure area, and the zone transitions to heritage preservation, recreation, and ecological accommodation. Front Street transforms from a vehicle-dominated commercial corridor into a continuous pedestrian promenade connecting heritage sites including the Banyan Tree, Moku'ula, and the harbor.
