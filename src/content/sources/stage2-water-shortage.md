The thesis identifies the Stage 2 Water Shortage declaration as the most immediate constraint on recovery. West Maui's Maximum Reliable Capacity is 4.26 million gallons per day, while projected demand for 2027 -- assuming rebuilding of lost homes and fulfillment of DHHL reservations -- is 6.04 MGD, a deficit of 1.78 MGD. This prohibits issuance of new water meters, acting as a hard brake on development beyond what was previously permitted. The thesis describes this as an absurd condition: fire survivors' destroyed homes no longer have active meters, and re-establishing service may count as new demand in a system with none to spare.
