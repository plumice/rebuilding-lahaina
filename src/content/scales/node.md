---
title: "Node"
summary: "Individual buildings and public spaces — the 26-foot module system and the elementary school as demonstration project."
---

At the site scale, the framework tests whether regional and urban decisions produce coherent architecture. The elementary school hub serves as the primary demonstration — a functional node combining educational program with emergency capacity at a location anchoring the redensification zone. The 26-foot module, derived from Hawaiian hale proportions and built in mass timber, is a kit-of-parts system supporting aggregation, repair, and expansion without full reconstruction. The framework does not prescribe form; it establishes relationships between systems that produce form when applied to specific sites and programs.
